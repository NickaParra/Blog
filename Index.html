Crime and Punishment

"Crime and Punishment" follows Rodion Raskolnikov, a destitute former student in St. Petersburg, who murders a pawnbroker to test his belief that some people have the right to commit crimes for a greater good. Consumed by guilt and paranoia, Raskolnikov struggles with his conscience and the psychological aftermath of his crime. He eventually confesses and is sentenced to Siberia, where he begins to seek redemption through the influence of Sonia Marmeladov, a compassionate woman who supports him.

The novel explores themes of morality, guilt, redemption, and the impact of poverty, highlighting the complexities of human nature and the possibility of salvation through suffering and love.
